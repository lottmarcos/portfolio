# Tone of Voice

Guidelines for all user-facing content in the portfolio — copy, labels, descriptions, error messages, and metadata.

## Voice Characteristics

| Attribute | Description | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Direct** | Lead with the point. No throat-clearing or filler. | "I build systems that scale." not "I'm passionate about building scalable systems." |
| **Confident, not arrogant** | State capabilities without hedging or boasting. | "Built the auth layer for 2M users." not "Had the privilege of contributing to..." |
| **Technical but accessible** | Use precise language without gatekeeping. | "Server Components reduce client bundle size." not "Leveraging cutting-edge RSC paradigms." |
| **Restrained** | Say less, mean more. Every word earns its place. | Short paragraphs. No walls of text. |

## Anti-Patterns

- No buzzword soup ("leverage", "synergy", "cutting-edge", "passionate").
- No false humility ("I was lucky to...", "I had the privilege of...").
- No hype language ("revolutionary", "game-changing", "10x").
- No emoji in professional copy (acceptable in casual/social contexts only).
- No exclamation marks in headings or CTAs.

## Language

- Default language: English (the portfolio targets an international audience).
- Write in first person where appropriate ("I built...", "My approach...").
- Use active voice.
- Prefer short sentences. Break long sentences at the conjunction.

## Applying the Tone

- **Project descriptions**: Technical and specific. What it does, what decisions were made, what the outcome was.
- **About section**: Personal but not informal. Show the human behind the engineer.
- **Navigation and labels**: Minimal. One or two words per label.
- **Error states**: Helpful and honest. "Page not found." not "Oops! We can't find that page!"
