# Tone of Voice

Guidelines for all user-facing content in the portfolio — copy, labels, descriptions, error messages, and metadata.

## The Core Voice

Marcos's voice is **calmly confident, warm, and honest**. It earns authority by being specific and by telling the truth about the journey — not by posturing. The strongest signal is a senior engineer who has nothing to prove and is glad to help others find their way.

Two modes coexist:

- **The thesis** (hero, headlines, project descriptions, microcopy): direct, restrained, every word earns its place.
- **The story** (About/bio, footer): a genuine narrative arc — where it started, the turning point, where it's going — told with composure, not drama.

## Voice Characteristics

| Attribute | Description | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Direct** | Lead with the point. No filler. | "I build high-trust interfaces." |
| **Confident, not arrogant** | State capability through evidence, not adjectives. | "UCITS Web generated $161k in commissions in four months." not "I'm an expert in fintech." |
| **Warm and human** | First person, speaks with the reader. Composed, never gushing. | "And what I learn along the way, I share with other developers." |
| **Honest about the journey** | Willing to show the turning point and the work behind the growth. | "But it wasn't always like this." |
| **Technical but accessible** | Precise without gatekeeping. | "Real-time data, typed state, explicit trade-offs." |
| **Restrained** | Say less, mean more — especially in microcopy. | Short paragraphs. No walls of text. |

## The About Narrative (canonical arc)

The About/bio is the one place where a deliberate narrative arc is not only allowed but expected:

1. **Now** — who I am and the scale I work at (with proof: $620M+ monthly volume, 200k+ orders).
2. **"But it wasn't always like this"** — the honest struggle: measuring myself by speed, feeling like a ticket-closer more than an engineer.
3. **The turning point** — speed only compounds with judgment; from writing code to designing systems (UCITS Web as proof).
4. **Today** — AI as leverage, care for the details, and a quiet line about helping other developers.

Metrics stay — they are strong proof — but they live inside the story, not as a flex list.

## Anti-Patterns

- No buzzword soup ("synergy", "cutting-edge", "passionate about technology").
- No false humility ("I was lucky to...", "I had the privilege of...").
- No hype language ("revolutionary", "game-changing", "10x").
- No exclamation marks in headings or CTAs.
- Emoji: sparingly, and only in casual microcopy (footer credit, error/visitor states). Never in headlines or project descriptions.
- The "help other developers" mission is a quiet thread, not a pillar — one honest line, never a sales pitch. Marcos is an engineer who shares, not an influencer.

## Language

- Default language: English (international audience). Portuguese is a full peer locale and must carry the same arc and warmth, not a literal translation.
- First person, active voice.
- Prefer short sentences. Break long sentences at the conjunction.
- The em dash (—) is the house punctuation for the warm aside.

## Applying the Tone

- **Hero / headlines**: the thesis. Direct and strong; the warmth lives in the supporting tagline.
- **About / bio**: the story. Personal, honest, the full arc. Personal but not informal.
- **Contact / footer**: warm invitation. Recruiter-facing first, with a quiet welcome to developers (product, systems, or career).
- **Timeline**: light narrative thread. Factual entries with a human touch, never inflated.
- **Navigation and labels**: minimal. One or two words per label.
- **Error states**: helpful and honest. "Page not found." not "Oops! We can't find that page!"
